A night out the way it was always supposed to be — members only, exceptionally fun, and owned by the people in the room.

Friday and Saturday nights at 6 Minutes are not open to the public. You're here because you're a member — which means everyone around you is too. The people on the dancefloor, behind the bar, in the kitchen: all owners. All with a stake in making this the best night you've had in years.

When everyone owns the place, something shifts. People look out for each other. The food is cooked with care because nobody's cutting corners for a margin. The air is good. The music is chosen by someone who actually gives a damn. You make real friends, because the person next to you isn't just passing through — they helped build this.

"Good air, good food, good people. Just the way it was always supposed to be."

During the week, the same building becomes something quieter: workshops and classes run by working practitioners — ceramics, cooking, music production, writing — and a co-working space for people who work better when they're around interesting people. One membership. One community. Seven days a week.

This only works if it's genuinely owned by the people in it. Not a landlord in Monaco. Not a private equity fund that bought the building ten years ago. The people who show up — who dance here on Saturday and sit here on Tuesday — hold the shares, make the decisions, and share in what it earns. Community-owned from day one, or not at all.

Founding members get

  • Members-only Friday & Saturday nights — never open to the public
  • Actual equity — a share in the building and what it earns
  • Vote on music, programming, food, and the direction of the space
  • Founding-rate membership pricing, locked in permanently
  • Access to the space before the public opening
